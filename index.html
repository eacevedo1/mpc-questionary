Auditory Perception Study

Invitation to Participate

You are invited to take part in a short study about auditory perception. The goal is to understand how people perceive small changes in musical pitch in different listening contexts.

You will listen to short audio examples and make simple judgments about whether two tones are the same or different. The experiment takes about 8–12 minutes.

There are no right or wrong answers—we are interested in your perceptual sensitivity. Your participation is voluntary and you may stop at any time.

To complete the study, please use headphones and choose a quiet environment.

Task 1: Pitch Discrimination for Isolated Tones

Listen carefully and respond

You will hear two short pure tones.

Please decide whether the second tone is the same or slightly different from the first.

Sometimes there is no change; sometimes the second tone is slightly higher or lower.

Respond after each pair by choosing:

  • "Same"
  • "Different (higher)"
  • "Different (lower)"

This task establishes your personal pitch-sensitivity baseline.

Task 2: Pitch Discrimination in a Familiar Melody

"Happy Birthday"

You will hear short excerpts of the melody "Happy Birthday."

Two tones inside the melody may be slightly altered in pitch.

After each example, indicate whether you heard a pitch change.

If you did hear a change, indicate whether the altered tone was higher or lower than normal.

Task 3: Pitch Discrimination in an Unfamiliar Melody

Listen carefully and respond

This task is identical to Task 2, but the melody will be unknown to you.

Two tones may be slightly mistuned.

Indicate whether you heard a change, and whether it was higher or lower.
